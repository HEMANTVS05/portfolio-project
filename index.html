Research & PhD Work

PhD Research Work

Title: Efficient Decentralized Routing using Multi-Agent Systems in VANETS

Vehicular ADHOC Network (VANET) is a kind of advanced MANET specially designed for vehicular communication. VANETs have the possibility to integrate with Wi-Fi, Bluetooth and other connectivity protocols which make it a new standard. Traffic congestion is based on a condition of road networks that may cause slow speed the vehicle move, longer trip timing changes, and vehicle queue length increase. Accurate predictions need for accurate status information about vehicles - the thing is that the distributed form of vehicles for massive road infrastructure that creates mostly challenging one. By using real time traffic information the advanced vehicle guidance systems can be done but unfortunately can only react with the presence of traffic. Since vehicular communication is a part of constantly changing environment and it must also operate in urban and rural areas.

This research work introduces a multi-agent system approach to solve the problems faced by vehicular communications since it comprises of more than one agent solve the problem of routing, the resulting protocol gives higher efficiency and better performance. In this system a novel approach is proposed for choosing the Cluster Head (CH). The CH selection algorithm is done in three levels, first level normal CH selection, and second level is cluster leaving and finally clusters merging. After choosing the CH, the routing path to be established based on the link quality which is measured using the link life time. Routing in dynamic environment is difficult therefore Multi agent based cooperative routing in dynamic vehicular technology is proposed. The objective of this work is to determine effective routing by utilizing the information provided by the intelligent multi agent systems.

A Co-operative Cluster based Multi-Agent Approach (CCMAA) for intelligent routing protocol is proposed for better results since implementing traffic forecasting and efficient routing in dynamically changing environment is a challenging task. The resulting protocol gives higher efficiency and better performance on packet delivery rate, end-to-end delay and throughput. The results are promising and proved to outperform the simulation tool in terms of network lifetime, energy-efficient routing and packet delivery ratio.
